Flavors of the South

Chilean gastronomy is a fusion of indigenous traditions and European influence. From the world-famous Cabernet Sauvignon of the Central Valley to the fresh ceviche of the Pacific coast, every dish tells a story of the land and sea.

CAZUELA DE VACUNO

Cazuela de Vacuno is a traditional Chilean beef stew that embodies comfort and home-style cooking, made with tender cuts of beef simmered alongside potatoes, pumpkin, corn on the cob, carrots, and green beans in a flavorful broth. This hearty dish is seasoned simply with garlic, onion, and herbs, allowing the natural flavors of the fresh vegetables and meat to shine. Served piping hot, often with a sprinkle of fresh parsley, it’s not just a nourishing meal but also a cultural staple—bringing families together around the table and reflecting Chile’s rich culinary heritage rooted in wholesome, rustic ingredients.

CHILEAN WINE

Tour the heart of Chile’s wine country, where centuries-old traditions meet modern innovation. In the Maipo Valley, bold Cabernet Sauvignon thrives under the warm sun, while the coastal breezes of San Antonio nurture elegant Syrah and crisp Sauvignon Blanc. The Colchagua Valley offers lush Carmenère, Chile’s signature grape, alongside boutique wineries that blend heritage with artistry. Visitors can stroll through vineyards framed by the majestic Andes, join harvest festivals like the Fiesta de la Vendimia, and taste wines that capture the soul of Chile’s diverse terroir. With its world-class routes and unforgettable pairings, Chile is a true paradise for wine lovers.

CHILEAN EMPANADA

No journey through Chile is complete without savoring an Empanada de Pino, the country’s most beloved comfort food. Traditionally baked in wood-fire ovens, this golden pastry is filled with a savory mix of seasoned beef, onions, olives, raisins, and a slice of hard-boiled egg — a recipe that balances sweet and salty flavors in perfect harmony. Found in every village and city corner, from bustling Santiago markets to coastal towns, the empanada is more than a snack; it is a cultural symbol enjoyed during national holidays, family gatherings, and festivals. Whether paired with a glass of Chilean wine or eaten fresh from a street vendor, the empanada embodies the warmth and authenticity of Chilean gastronomy.
