UNESCO World Heritage

Sewell Mining Town

Sewell Mining Town, nestled in the Andes near Rancagua, Chile, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that once thrived as a bustling copper mining settlement. It is renowned for its striking hillside layout and colorful wooden buildings.

The City of Stairs:

Built on a slope too steep for vehicles, the town is connected entirely by stairs, creating a unique urban footprint in the mountains.
