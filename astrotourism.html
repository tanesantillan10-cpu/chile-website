ELQUI VALLEY

Location: Coquimbo

The Elqui Valley in northern Chile is a breathtaking region celebrated for its crystal-clear skies, making it one of the world’s premier destinations for stargazing and astronomy. Nestled between desert mountains and fertile riverbanks, the valley blends natural beauty with cultural richness, offering visitors lush vineyards that produce fine wines and pisco, charming villages with authentic local traditions, and serene landscapes perfect for relaxation. Its unique combination of astronomy, agriculture, and rural charm makes the Elqui Valley a place where science, culture, and nature harmoniously converge.

SAN PEDRO DE ATACAMA

Location: Valle de la Luna

San Pedro de Atacama is a small desert town in northern Chile that serves as the gateway to some of the country’s most extraordinary landscapes. Surrounded by salt flats, geysers, volcanoes, and high-altitude lagoons, it offers visitors breathtaking views of the Andes and the Atacama Desert’s unique terrain. Known for its crystal-clear skies, it is also one of the best places in the world for stargazing, with observatories and night tours showcasing the Milky Way in dazzling detail. Combining indigenous culture, natural wonders, and astronomical experiences, San Pedro de Atacama is a destination where adventure and discovery meet in the heart of the desert.

Windows to the Universe

From the crystal-clear skies of the Atacama Desert to the high-altitude observatories of the Andes, Chile is the world’s capital of stargazing. With over 300 cloudless nights a year, the country offers unmatched views of the Milky Way, planets, and distant galaxies. Visitors can tour cutting-edge observatories, join guided night-sky experiences, or simply marvel at the brilliance of the cosmos from desert valleys and mountain plateaus. With its unique geography and pristine skies, Chile is the ultimate destination for astrotourism.
