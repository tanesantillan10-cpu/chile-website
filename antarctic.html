The Gateway: Magallanes

Punta Arenas

Punta Arenas, the southernmost city on mainland Chile, sits on the shores of the Strait of Magellan and serves as a vibrant gateway to Patagonia and Antarctica. Known for its rich maritime history, European-influenced architecture, and colorful houses, the city blends cultural heritage with natural wonders.

Exploration:

Visitors can explore nearby penguin colonies, windswept landscapes, and historic sites while enjoying its role as a hub for scientific exploration.

The Frontier: South Shetland Islands

King George Island

The largest of the South Shetland Islands, King George Island is a hub of international scientific research. Located 120 km from the Antarctic Peninsula, it hosts more than a dozen research stations operated by countries such as Chile, Russia, and China.

Wildlife:

Despite its harsh climate, penguins, seals, and seabirds thrive along its icy shores, symbolizing global cooperation in extreme environments.
