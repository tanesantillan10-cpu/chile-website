From Altiplano to Austral

Spanning over 4,000 kilometers, Chile’s protected wilderness offers a spectacular study in contrast. From the high-altitude volcanic silence of Lauca in the north to the windswept, penguin-inhabited isles of Magdalena in the south, these sanctuaries preserve the rarest ecosystems on our planet.

LAUCA NATIONAL PARK

Lauca National Park, nestled in Chile’s northern Altiplano near the Bolivian border, is a breathtaking high-altitude sanctuary of snowcapped volcanoes, shimmering lakes, and vast puna grasslands. Centered around the dazzling Chungará Lake—one of the world’s highest—it offers dramatic views of Parinacota and Pomerape volcanoes while sheltering diverse wildlife such as vicuñas, alpacas, and flamingos. Rich in Andean culture and natural beauty, Lauca is a destination where visitors can experience both the serenity of remote landscapes and the vibrant life of unique ecosystems.

MAGDALENA ISLAND

Magdalena Island, located in the Strait of Magellan near Punta Arenas, is one of Chile’s most remarkable wildlife sanctuaries, home to over 120,000 Magellanic penguins that nest along its shores. This protected area offers visitors an unforgettable experience of walking among vast penguin colonies, accompanied by seabirds such as cormorants and gulls. Accessible by boat, the island provides a rare opportunity to observe these charismatic creatures up close in their natural habitat, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.
